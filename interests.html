Professional Interests

Mechatronics

I got my first taste of robotics in elementary school, where we competed in the state FIRST Lego League. Ever since then, I've enjoyed tinkering with parts and designing simple systems. I'm currently doing research on foot sensors for robot locomotion at MIT.

Systems Engineering

Large scale projects have many moving parts, all of which have to work together in a predictable and seamless manner. While interning at JPL, I realized how important it was for dedicated engineers to manage requirements and ensure that every system does what it's designed to do.

Space Exploration

As a child, I watched documentaries and TV shows about space that instilled in me a deep sense of wonder. Other space-themed media stoked that interest, leading me to persue an internship at NASA. Space is the final frontier and I would love to play a part in revealing its secrets.

AI and Cognition

One of my favorite fields is the philosophy of intelligence and cognition. At MIT, I've had the fortune of taking philosophical and technical classes on machines and AI. I believe that not only is there nothing preventing a piece of silicon from becoming intelligent, it's going to happen in my lifetime.

Data Science

Humans may be pattern recognizing machines, but we are very bad at analyzing high dimensional data. However, humans and machines combined have been shown to far outperform individuals. I especially enjoy digging into data using machine tools and finding relationships that aren't obvious.

Finance

Having designed a CPU, I've seen firsthand that a collection of simple systems can give rise to complex emergent properties and the markets are just that. Moreover, money is the foundation of modern society. I want to understand the structures behind money and uncover the market's patterns.

Just For Fun

Rocketry

Rockets are fast, loud, and sometimes dangerous. Like cars but better in every way! (Don't let MIT motorsports hear that.) Through soaring heights and explosive setbacks, I continue to believe that the Rocket Team will reach space before I graduate.

Building Computers

Tinkering with PCs requires finding a balance between power, thermals, and noise. I've taken to designing small form factor computers: how do you stuff the maximum amount of computing power in the smallest possible package? The answer may include a dremel.

Sci-Fi Television

Whether it's the wide-eyed optimism of Star Trek, the gritty realism of The Expanse, or the bleak pessimism of Black Mirror, science fiction captures my attention like nothing else. My favorite show is CBS's Person of Interest, with its prescient depiction of surveillance and AI.

Sailing

The Charles River cuts through the center of Greater Boston and is a focal point of summertime recreation. While I am by no means a master helmsman, there's nothing like floating on a boat between classes to relieve stress.

Winter Sports

Having been raised in the Northeast, winter is my favorite season. It's no suprise that I enjoy all sorts of winter activities, from hockey to skiing to simply watching the snow fall. The one thing I don't enjoy is shoveling out the driveway.

PC Gaming

Although I have a wide variety of games in my Steam library, I especially enjoy strategy and simulation. On the off chance that I have free time, you can find me playing games like XCOM, Stellaris, or Kerbal Space Program.
